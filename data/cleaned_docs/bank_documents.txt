Document 4: Online Banking Security Tips
**Document ID:** BA-OS-004
**Effective Date:** 2026-04-01
Protecting Your SecureBank Online Accounts
At SecureBank, your security is our top priority. Follow these essential tips to keep your online banking experience safe and secure.
General Security Practices:
**Strong Passwords:** Create unique, complex passwords for your online banking account. Use a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using easily guessable information like birthdays or names.
**Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Always enable 2FA for an extra layer of security. This requires a second verification step, such as a code sent to your mobile phone, in addition to your password.
**Monitor Account Activity:** Regularly review your account statements and transaction history for any suspicious activity. Report unauthorized transactions immediately.
**Secure Wi-Fi:** Only access your online banking through secure, private Wi-Fi networks. Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions.
Phishing and Scams:
**Be Skeptical:** Be wary of unsolicited emails, text messages, or phone calls asking for personal or account information. SecureBank will never ask for your full password, PIN, or one-time passcodes via email or text.
**Verify Links:** Before clicking on any links, hover over them to see the actual URL. Ensure it leads to the official SecureBank website.
**Report Suspicious Activity:** If you receive a suspicious communication claiming to be from SecureBank, do not respond. Forward the email to `phishing@securebank.com` or call our customer support line directly.

Applying for a mortgage can be a complex process, but SecureBank is here to guide you every step of the way. This document outlines the general steps involved in securing a home loan with us.

Step 2: Find a Home & Make an Offer
Once pre-approved, you can confidently search for a home within your budget. When your offer is accepted, you'll move to the next stage.
Step 3: Complete the Mortgage Application
Submit a full mortgage application with all required documentation, including:
Recent pay stubs and W-2 forms.
Bank and investment statements.
Tax returns for the past two years.
Details of any other debts.
Step 4: Underwriting
Our underwriting team will review your application, credit history, and property appraisal to assess risk and ensure all criteria are met.
Step 5: Closing
Upon final approval, you'll sign the necessary legal documents and the loan will be disbursed. Congratulations, you're a homeowner!
Document 6: Fraud Prevention and Reporting
**Document ID:** BA-FP-006
**Effective Date:** 2026-06-01
SecureBank Fraud Prevention and Reporting Guidelines
SecureBank is committed to protecting you from fraud. Understanding common fraud schemes and knowing how to report suspicious activity are crucial for your financial safety.
Common Fraud Types:
**Phishing:** Attempts to trick you into revealing personal information (passwords, account numbers) through fake emails, texts, or websites.
**Identity Theft:** When someone uses your personal information without your permission to commit fraud.
**Scams:** Various schemes designed to trick you into sending money or providing financial details, often involving false promises or urgent requests.
How to Protect Yourself:
**Be Vigilant:** Always question unexpected requests for money or personal information.
**Secure Your Devices:** Use antivirus software, firewalls, and keep your operating system and applications updated.
**Review Statements:** Check your bank and credit card statements regularly for unauthorized transactions.
**Shred Documents:** Properly dispose of sensitive documents by shredding them.
Reporting Fraud:
If you suspect you have been a victim of fraud or notice suspicious activity on your SecureBank accounts:
**Contact SecureBank Immediately:** Call our dedicated Fraud Hotline at 1-800-555-FRUD (1-800-555-3783) or visit your nearest branch.
**Change Passwords:** Update your online banking passwords and any other compromised credentials.
**File a Police Report:** For identity theft or significant financial loss, file a report with your local law enforcement agency.
**Notify Credit Bureaus:** Consider placing a fraud alert or freezing your credit with major credit bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion).

**Document ID:** BA-WT-007

SecureBank International Wire Transfer Services
SecureBank provides secure and efficient international wire transfer services for sending funds globally. This guide outlines the process and requirements.
Initiating a Wire Transfer:
International wire transfers can be initiated through our online banking portal or by visiting a SecureBank branch. For security reasons, first-time international transfers may require in-person verification.
Required Information for Recipient:
To ensure a smooth transfer, you will need the following details for the recipient:
Full Name and Address of Recipient.
Recipient's Bank Name and Address.
Recipient's Account Number.
SWIFT/BIC Code of the Recipient's Bank.
IBAN (International Bank Account Number) for transfers to Europe.
Fees and Exchange Rates:
**Outgoing International Wire Fee:** $45 per transfer.
**Incoming International Wire Fee:** $15 per transfer.
**Exchange Rates:** SecureBank uses competitive exchange rates, which are displayed at the time of transfer initiation. Rates are subject to change.
Processing Times:
International wire transfers typically take 1-5 business days to be received by the beneficiary bank, depending on the destination country and intermediary banks involved. Transfers initiated after 3:00 PM EST will be processed on the next business day.
Important Considerations:
**Verification:** For your security, large transfers or transfers to new recipients may require additional verification steps.
**Irrevocable:** Once an international wire transfer is sent, it is generally irrevocable. Please ensure all recipient details are accurate before confirming the transfer.
**Regulations:** All international transfers are subject to anti-money laundering (AML) and other regulatory compliance checks.
